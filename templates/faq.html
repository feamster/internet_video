What Is Our Research?

In this research, you will watch and rate the videos. The purpose of this research project was to automatically detect the quality preferences of the users. By the preferences, the internet video providers could provide better resource allocation scheme that can satisfy as many as users.

How Does It Work?

  1. If you agree to participate in our program, we will give you an online registration form by which you can have the access to our video proxy. After registration, you can watch the videos through our video proxy which allows us to monitor the quality of video.
  2. You will install an extension on web browser which will redirect your webpage to our web proxy if you click a online video link.
  3. You will watch the videos through our proxy where we record the quality of your perceived videos and the status of your video player. The data includes the playing progress of the video player, watching video in the fullscreen mode or not, how many seconds of the video have downloaded in your player buffer, the resolution of video you are watching, and your downloading status, and the pixel size of your player.
  4. You will rate the video that you are currently watching on the video player provided by our video proxy.
  5. After you watch a YouTube video, a webpage might be popped up, and you will ask the questions on that page including your rating to the video you just watch and the annoying factors that affect your experience. You will have an option to test your internet connection speed which will be recorded.
  6. You can decide to quit our study and delete all your related data at any time if you feel uncomfortable.

Data Privacy

By default, We stores data about the participants. In each video watching, the quality and the rating of the video is stored, it is sent to the secure servers at University of Chicago. Each participant is given a completely random id that is used to distinguish between participants. This random participant id allows us to anonymize the data and minimize risk to individual participants. We use a crosstalk system to store the mapping from participants' emails and names to the random ID, but this crosstalk system is only for contact use. After the study, we will completely destroy it. We will store the following information:
  • The Internet plan and the model of the monitor you are using.
  • The quality of the videos you watch including the video resolution, the video stall time, the player's buffer length.
  • The quality rating of the videos.
We will NOT store the following information:
  • The content of the videos you are watching.
  • The crosstalk system that maps your email and name to the ID.
  • Your name and e-mail address.
You can delete all the information we record any time. To make sure that this research is being carried out in the proper way, the University of Chicago IRB may review study records. The Office of Social and Behavioral Sciences may also look over study records during required reviews.
